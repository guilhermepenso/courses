Android Mascot History

You probably know that the Operational System Android, keep by Google is one of the most used for mobile devices worldwide. But you probably don't know that their kind mascot has a very interesting name and history? Then follow us through this article to learn a lot of things about this robot.

First Version

The first try to create a mascot came in 2007 from a developer called Dan Morrill. He tells that he opened Inkscape (Free software for vectoring images) and created his own robot version. His objective was to only personify the system for his team, without any company requesting for a mascot creation.

This bizarre first version was baptized as an tribute to it's creator: the Dandroids.

A New Mascot Arise

The idea to have a new mascot was maturing and the mission was passed to a professional in the area. The illustrator Russa Irina Blok, also working at Google, got the missions to represent the little robot in a more pleasant way.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything graphically with less traces and in a more plate shape. The drawing also should needed a fast and easy recognition by the look. And then was created the Bugdroid, the new Android's Mascot.

The main inspiration for the traces and shapes from Bugdroid was taken from the dolls that identify the genre in doors for bathrooms. Story told that the artist was creating from it's office on Google and looked to the bathroom, identifying it with ease, simple and clean from it's objective.

Want to learn more?

Another curious talk about Android is that each version has been always a tribute to some candy, in a alphabetical order since version 1.5 until 9.0.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 3.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lollipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, Android Q wasn't created, as Google ended this fun practice and began using numbers, giving birth to Android 10.

Access the Android History website to know the sequence of "sugary" versions and what new each one brought to the Android system.
